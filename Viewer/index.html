Analysis of Election Data

by Andrew Conway and Vanessa Teague

This site gives background details for our analysis of election outcomes. You can generally reproduce these results yourself using the programs in the git repository https://github.com/AndrewConway/ConcreteSTV or just quickly look at the results here.

2020 ACT election

In our report we show three classes of bugs that could in principle change election outcomes in the ACT vote counting code. These cropped up in the 2020 election. Elections ACT acknowledged two of the bug classes, and fixed all three and recounted the election in 2021.

Federal Senate elections 2013-2019

In our report we show one clear class of bug related to tiebreaking and two bizarre changes in count algorithm between the 2013, 2016 and 2019 elections. None of these impacted the choice of winner, but they did affect totals and could have impacted winners in future. The end result was a legislative change to bring the requirements into line with the AEC's implementation, and also to introduce an audit of the paper ballots. We are delighted that this law received multi-party support.

NSW Local Government election 2021

Our report examines the size of missing or altered votes that would be sufficient for iVote's security or performance issues to have impacted the election results. A companion web site details the examples from the paper.
